Utility Cost Calculator

What Do We Owe for Utilities?

Overview

This site calculates your utility usage and associated cost. It does so based on data that you must provide in a rigidly formatted JSON file. Any syntax errors will cause failure. See the accompanying 'readings.json' file; its syntax is fairly straight forward. The outer square brackets indicate a list and each comma separated component is a curly bracket enclosed collection of name/value pairs, also separated by comas. All names are in quotes. Values can be numbers or string literals wich must also be in quotes.

The Electricity Meter

Calculating the cost of electricity is complicated because of the way PG&E prices their product. It involves tier pricing with base levels varying by season and by region within the state (CA.) For now, the region which includes Bolinas is assumed and pricing is assumed to be that in effect at time of writing (mid 2016.)

The Gas Meter

Once the gas meter has been read, calculating the cost of what has been consumed is much easier since the only variable other than amount used is the cost (which has been quite volatile.) The one minor complication is that the meter reads cubic feet while price is by the gallon. Therefore a conversion factor (gallons per cubic foot) is needed.
